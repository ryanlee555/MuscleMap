SPLITS


Knowing which workouts to do comes with trial and error. The more important aspect about working out is to determine
which split you want to follow throughout your week. If you have more time on your hands and want to workout 5-6
times a week, you might want to consider the PPL, Arnold, or Bro Split. If you are busier (which is completely fine),
the upper/ lower body or full body split might be best for you. Finding the right split that fits into your schedule
is very important, and I will give you some sample workouts for each potential split. Again, there are an infinite
amount of customizable splits that you can do. These are just some of the most popular ones to help get you started
formulating your own one, and hopefully in the future, you will be able to customize your own split.




PPL


PPL stands for Push, Pull, Legs. Push indicates any muscle movements that require a pushing motion; this includes
chest, shoulders, and triceps. On the other hand, Pull indicates any muscle movements that require a pulling motion;
back and biceps. Push Pull Legs are considered one of the most popular workout splits, because it evenly hits each muscle

ARNOLD


The Arnold Split is a workout routine that was popularized by the king of bodybuilding himself, Arnold Schwarzenegger.
The workout consists of three separate days: chest and back, shoulders and arms, and legs. The Arnold split is another
very popular workout split, as you are able to hit all muscle groups consistently, similar to PPL. My personal split consists of
doing PPL on Mon-Wed, and Arnold on Thu-Sat, with a rest day on Sunday. These are some sample workouts that follow the Arnold split.
